## Export Documentation Requirements

Indian exporters must prepare the following documents for international shipments:

1. **Commercial Invoice** – Details the goods, quantity, value, and buyer/seller information.
2. **Packing List** – Itemizes each package in the shipment with weights and dimensions.
3. **Bill of Lading (B/L)** – Issued by the carrier as a contract of carriage and receipt of goods.
4. **Certificate of Origin (CoO)** – Certifies the country where the goods were manufactured. Required for preferential tariff treatment under FTAs.
5. **Shipping Bill** – Customs export declaration filed electronically via ICEGATE.
6. **Letter of Credit (LC)** – Bank-guaranteed payment instrument widely used in international trade.
7. **IEC (Import Export Code)** – 10-digit code issued by DGFT; mandatory for all Indian exporters.

## Key Export Markets for Indian Spices

### Germany
- Largest organic food market in Europe.
- High demand for certified organic spices (BIO certification preferred).
- Key buyers: Metro Group, Edeka, Lidl.
- Tariff: EU MFN rate ~5-9%; India-EU FTA negotiations ongoing.

### United States
- 3.5 million Indian-Americans create strong diaspora demand.
- FDA registration mandatory for food exporters.
- FSSAI-equivalent: FDA Food Safety Modernization Act (FSMA) compliance required.
- HS Code 0904: Pepper; 0910: Ginger, Saffron, Turmeric.

### United Arab Emirates
- Gateway to 54 OIC (Organisation of Islamic Cooperation) countries.
- Halal certification opens broader Gulf market access.
- Dubai Multi Commodities Centre (DMCC) is a key re-export hub.

### United Kingdom
- Post-Brexit: UK Global Tariff applies (no EU rates).
- UKCA marking not required for food products.
- British Retail Consortium (BRC) certification preferred by supermarkets.

## Export Compliance & Certifications

### APEDA (Agricultural & Processed Food Products Export Development Authority)
- Registration required for agricultural product exporters.
- Certifies: Basmati rice, spices, fresh fruits & vegetables, meat.

### FSSAI Export Certificate
- Food Safety and Standards Authority of India certificate.
- Required for exporting food products from India.

### BIS Certification
- Mandatory for certain electronic and consumer goods.

### Phytosanitary Certificate
- Issued by the Department of Agriculture.
- Required for plant-based products to confirm pest/disease-free status.

## Payment Terms in Export Trade

- **Advance Payment**: Seller ships after receiving full payment. Low risk for exporter.
- **Letter of Credit (LC)**: Bank guarantee; preferred for large transactions.
- **Documents Against Payment (DP)**: Buyer pays before receiving documents.
- **Open Account**: Goods shipped before payment; highest risk for exporter.
- **ECGC Cover**: Export Credit Guarantee Corporation protects against payment default.

## GST & Duty Drawback on Exports

- Exports are zero-rated under GST (IGST refundable).
- **Duty Drawback Scheme**: Refunds customs duty paid on imported inputs used in export goods.
- **RoDTEP** (Remission of Duties and Taxes on Exported Products): Refunds embedded taxes not covered by other schemes.
- Apply via ICEGATE portal: https://www.icegate.gov.in/
